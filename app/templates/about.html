What are Deepfakes?

Deepfakes are synthetic media where a person's likeness is replaced with someone else's using artificial intelligence. The technology uses deep learning algorithms to create realistic but fake images of people.

While deepfakes can be used for entertainment and creative purposes, they also pose serious concerns including:

  • Spreading misinformation and fake news
  • Creating convincing but false evidence
  • Identity theft and fraud
  • Harassment and defamation

Our Detection Method

This application uses advanced machine learning techniques to identify deepfake content:

LSTM Neural Networks

We use Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks which are particularly effective at analyzing facial features and patterns. Our model has been trained on a diverse dataset of real and fake images to accurately identify manipulated content.

Facial Feature Analysis

Our system detects faces in images and extracts key features. These features are then analyzed for patterns that indicate manipulation, such as unnatural blending boundaries or inconsistent facial features.

Limitations

While our system uses state-of-the-art techniques, it's important to understand its limitations:

  • Currently supports only image analysis (JPG, PNG formats)
  • Works best with clear, well-lit facial images
  • May occasionally produce false positives or negatives
  • Requires a clear view of the face for best results

Always use this tool as one part of your evaluation process, not as the sole determinant of authenticity.

Responsible Use

This tool is provided for educational and verification purposes. We encourage responsible use:

  • Do not use detection results to harass or unfairly discredit individuals
  • Be aware that even authentic content can be flagged as suspicious
  • Consider the context and source of media before making judgments
  • Share this tool to help others verify suspicious content
